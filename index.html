Integrated AI Interview & Data Platform

Goal: Design and build an end-to-end system that conducts interviews via VoIP and text, escalates to humans when needed, then transcribes, summarises, contextualises, and securely stores data for analysis (e.g., AI-assisted thematic analysis). Reduce manual fragmentation, improve patient experience, and strengthen data quality and governance.

Project 1 — Mapping Current Interview & Data Collection Processes

Overview

Establish ground truth across hospitals/clinics: who collects what, via which channels (phone, in-person, online forms), how consent is captured, where data goes, and how it is later used. Produce as-is maps, pain points, compliance risks, and a prioritised to-be design.

Phase: Discovery (Months 0–3) Methods: SLR · Field study · Process mining Outputs: Maps · Gaps · Requirements

Objectives

  • Document end-to-end interview and follow-up workflows by persona (nurse, admin, clinician, researcher).
  • Identify fragmentation, delays, re-work, and compliance exposure (privacy, consent, retention).
  • Define measurable KPIs (time-to-contact, completion rate, escalation %).
  • Derive functional & non-functional requirements for the integrated platform.

Methodology

  • Systematic Literature Review on healthcare interviewing, digital consent, and data governance.
  • Semi-structured interviews with staff; contextual inquiry and shadowing.
  • Process mining from call logs/CRM to surface bottlenecks and re-work loops.
  • BPMN mapping of as-is and to-be processes with RACI.

Planned Outcomes

  • Consolidated requirements (functional, security, privacy, interoperability).
  • Prioritised backlog for MVP and pilot sites.
  • Validated to-be designs with stakeholders and risk sign-off.

Milestones

  • M1: Stakeholder map & ethics approvals.
  • M2: As-is maps; pain-point inventory; baseline KPIs.
  • M3: To-be designs; MVP backlog & acceptance criteria.

Risks & Mitigations

  • Access to staff/time → schedule micro-sessions; asynchronous validation.
  • Hidden shadow processes → triangulate with logs and artefacts.
  • Scope creep → MoSCoW prioritisation; governance cadence.

Project 2 — Automated Interview Agent (Voice + Text)

Overview

Design a safe, effective AI agent to handle inbound/outbound VoIP calls, SMS, and web chat. The agent triages, captures consent, schedules or reschedules appointments, and escalates to humans when confidence is low or risk is high.

Phase: Build (Months 4–9) Channels: SIP/VoIP · WebRTC · SMS · Email Outcomes: MVP bot + policies

Objectives

  • Achieve high task completion without compromising safety or consent integrity.
  • Support multilingual prompts and accessibility (e.g., TTY/TDD, screen reader friendly chat).
  • Integrate with scheduling systems (calendar APIs) and send confirmations via SMS/email.
  • Define and test escalation thresholds and operator handover UX.

Methodology

  • Design Science iterative builds; UCD with patients and staff.
  • Conversation design with safety guardrails (do-not-answer lists, clinical boundaries).
  • A/B tests on prompts; confidence-aware fallback; real-time human takeover.
  • Metrics: containment rate, first-contact resolution, user satisfaction (CSAT), average handle time.

Planned Outcomes

  • Working prototype integrated with SIP/WebRTC and SMS gateways.
  • Documented escalation playbook and consent scripts.
  • Evaluation report with success thresholds for pilot rollout.

Risks & Mitigations

  • Misclassification → conservative thresholds; human review; post-call QA.
  • Latency/jitter in VoIP → edge media servers; QoS; fall back to call-back.
  • Consent ambiguity → explicit confirmations; recorded consent artefacts.

Milestones

  • M1: Dialogue flows; consent & safety policies.
  • M2: VoIP/SMS integration; scheduling; email follow-ups.
  • M3: Pilot-ready MVP; evaluation KPIs met.

Project 3 — Transcription, Summarisation & Contextualisation Pipeline

Overview

Build a clinical-grade pipeline: speech-to-text (STT) with medical vocabulary, speaker diarisation, structured summaries, and context merges (appointment metadata, EMR snippets) with provenance.

Phase: Build (Months 4–9) Outcomes: Production-grade pipeline

Objectives

  • Minimise word error rate (WER) on domain audio (accents, clinical terms).
  • Generate faithful, attributed summaries; no hallucinations; show citations to transcript spans.
  • Support email delivery of visit summaries to authorised recipients with consent.

Methodology

  • Benchmark multiple STT models; fine-tune lexicons; noise-robust preprocessing.
  • Prompting/evaluation harness for summary quality (factuality, coverage, style constraints).
  • Human QA sampling; red-team tests on sensitive prompts; privacy overlay for PII masking.

Planned Outcomes

  • Modular pipeline (STT → diarise → segment → summarise → contextualise → store).
  • Quality dashboards: WER, ROUGE-L, factuality, latency, cost per hour.
  • Schema for transcripts, summary JSON, and provenance links.

Risks & Mitigations

  • Domain terminology errors → domain lexicon; post-edit queue.
  • Privacy leakage → on-prem or private inference; PII masking; strict access controls.
  • Latency under load → batching/streaming; GPU autoscaling; async email delivery.

Milestones

  • M1: Baseline STT benchmark & target WER.
  • M2: Summarisation harness with fidelity checks.
  • M3: Context merge & schema; pilot integration.

Project 4 — Secure Data Platform & Governance

Overview

Design a secure platform for storing transcripts, summaries, artefacts, and analytics while meeting privacy regulations (e.g., Australian Privacy Act, HIPAA/GDPR contexts). Implement consent, identity, encryption, RBAC, and full auditability.

Phase: Platform (Months 6–24) Outcomes: Governance + blueprint

Objectives

  • Single, secure source of truth for interview data and derived artefacts.
  • Data lifecycle: consent, retention, deletion, and subject access requests (SAR).
  • Defensible security posture aligned to NIST CSF/ISO 27001; threat-modelled (STRIDE).

Methodology

  • Security architecture with zero trust principles; encryption in transit/at rest; KMS.
  • Privacy Impact Assessment; Data Protection Impact Assessment; data minimisation.
  • RBAC/ABAC design; tamper-evident audit logs; break-glass procedures.

Planned Outcomes

  • Reference architecture (cloud/on-prem), IaC blueprints, and runbooks.
  • Governance policies (consent, retention, classification, incident response).
  • Compliance mapping to regulatory obligations and hospital policies.

Risks & Mitigations

  • Over-permissive access → least privilege, just-in-time access, continuous review.
  • Regulatory drift → periodic compliance reviews; policy as code.
  • Vendor lock-in → open schemas, export pipelines, and abstraction layers.

Milestones

  • M1: Threat model & PIA/DPIA; control baseline.
  • M2: Reference architecture & IaC; audit log pipeline.
  • M3: Policy set ratified; access reviews in place.

Project 5 — AI-Assisted Thematic Analysis

Overview

Develop a research tool that assists human analysts to code transcripts, suggest themes, and maintain a transparent, reproducible codebook with provenance to raw text. Emphasis on auditability and inter-rater reliability.

Phase: Pilot (Months 9–15) Outcomes: Analyst workspace + studies

Objectives

  • Speed up coding without reducing rigour; maintain researcher agency.
  • Support active learning: model suggests codes; humans accept/edit; model improves.
  • Provide exports (CSV/JSON) for further statistics or SEM/qualitative tools.

Methodology

  • Qualitative methods (e.g., Braun & Clarke) integrated with ML (topic modelling, embeddings).
  • Measure inter-rater reliability (e.g., Cohen’s κ); usability tests; workload (NASA-TLX).
  • Ethics: minimise bias; transparent suggestions; enable contestability and appeals.

Planned Outcomes

  • Web workspace for coding, codebook management, and provenance trails.
  • Evaluation study showing time savings vs quality; reproducible pipeline.
  • Guidelines for safe, valid AI assistance in qualitative research.

Risks & Mitigations

  • Automation bias → emphasise human authority; show confidence and rationales.
  • Theme drift over time → versioned codebooks; frozen baselines for comparability.
  • Privacy of sensitive text → in-platform de-identification; secure enclaves.

Milestones

  • M1: Prototype coding UI + embeddings search.
  • M2: Active-learning loop; kappa monitoring.
  • M3: Research study & publication draft.

Project 6 — Human-in-the-Loop Orchestration & Escalation

Overview

Design service orchestration that routes tasks between AI and humans with clear SLAs, operator tools, and safety policies. Provide consoles for supervisors and clinicians to monitor queues and take over calls or chats seamlessly.

Phase: Pilot→Scale (Months 9–24) Outcomes: Orchestrator + Console

Objectives

  • Define escalation triggers (low confidence, safety phrases, clinical boundaries, consent issues).
  • Enable one-click human takeover with context transfer; record outcomes.
  • Provide dashboards: backlog, SLA breaches, safety incidents, operator utilisation.

Methodology

  • Workflow engine (e.g., BPMN) for routes and timers; policy-as-code for guardrails.
  • Service design with patients/clinicians; incident post-mortems; playbook drills.
  • Simulation & A/B tests to optimise thresholds and staffing models.

Planned Outcomes

  • Operator console and supervisor dashboard; audit trails for all actions.
  • Escalation playbooks and standard operating procedures (SOPs).
  • Readiness checklist for scale-out to multiple sites.

Risks & Mitigations

  • Alert fatigue → smarter triggers; noise budgets; tiered escalation.
  • Hand-off friction → rich context pass; pre-brief summaries.
  • Operational drift → scheduled reviews; continuous training; QA sampling.

Milestones

  • M1: Escalation policy & simulator.
  • M2: Console MVP; SLA monitors.
  • M3: Multi-site pilot; scale playbook.
